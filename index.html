Frequently asked questions

Know what your signature means.

Does signing automatically remove a politician?

No. It adds a signed petition record. INEC still conducts the official verification and any referendum required by law.

Who can be the subject of a recall?

The platform covers recall-eligible legislative offices such as Senate, House of Representatives and State House of Assembly seats.

Will my name appear publicly?

No. Public voter lists are anonymized. Full identity details are reserved for authorized petition preparation.

Why is a signature required?

A campaign supporter must affirm the petition and provide a record that can be arranged for verification—not merely click a reaction button.

Terms & Conditions

These terms govern the use of RecallNG to organize constituency records for a proposed legislative recall. By supporting a campaign, you agree to the following conditions.

1. Eligibility and truthful information

  • You must be at least 18 years old and provide only your own accurate voter information.
  • You may support only a campaign within the constituency or legislative jurisdiction in which you are registered.
  • You must not impersonate another voter, submit duplicate support, manipulate campaign totals or provide a false signature.

2. Nature of platform support

A signature recorded on RecallNG is a petition-support record. It does not itself recall a legislator, constitute INEC verification or replace any official referendum, biometric verification or other legal requirement.

3. Authorization

You authorize your submitted details and signature to be included in the relevant campaign’s protected petitioners’ schedule and, where lawfully required, presented to authorized campaign organizers, legal reviewers or INEC for verification and submission.

4. Lawful and responsible use

You must not use RecallNG to threaten, harass, defame, discriminate, upload unlawful material or interfere with the platform. Campaign allegations remain the responsibility of their authors and should be supported by verifiable evidence.

5. Accuracy and availability

Representative and electoral information is compiled from available official records. RecallNG may correct data, moderate unlawful content or suspend abusive activity. The service is provided as civic-organizing infrastructure and is not legal advice.

6. Changes

These terms may be updated to reflect legal, security or platform changes. Material updates should be displayed before further consent is collected.

NDPR / Nigeria Data Protection Act notice

Privacy Notice

This notice explains how RecallNG handles personal data in connection with campaign support, consistently with the Nigeria Data Protection Regulation 2019 and the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023.

1. Data collected

RecallNG may collect your name, VIN, date of birth, phone number, state, LGA or Area Council, ward, polling unit, signature, consent record and campaign participation.

2. Why the data is used

  • To determine whether you belong to the relevant constituency.
  • To prevent duplicate or unauthorized petition support.
  • To arrange petitioners by electoral geography and prepare campaign records for review and official verification.
  • To produce public campaign totals and analytics without publicly displaying your sensitive identity fields.

3. Legal basis and consent

Data submitted through the support form is processed with your affirmative consent and for the specific civic purpose described here. You may decline by not submitting the form.

4. Storage and disclosure

In this browser-based build, voter and campaign records are stored locally in your browser unless they are exported or deliberately shared. Petition records may be disclosed only to authorized campaign personnel, professional advisers, service providers acting under appropriate safeguards, INEC or another recipient required by law. RecallNG must not sell voter data or use it for unrelated political targeting.

5. Security and retention

Reasonable technical and organizational safeguards should be used to protect exported or deployed records. Personal data should be retained only as long as necessary for the campaign, verification, legal obligations and dispute handling, then securely deleted or anonymized.

6. Your rights

Subject to applicable law, you may request access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection, portability or withdrawal of consent. Withdrawal does not invalidate processing already lawfully completed and may be limited where records must be preserved for a legal claim or official petition process.

7. Regulatory information

Learn more from the Nigeria Data Protection Commission’s official NDP Act resource. The operator of any public RecallNG deployment is responsible for publishing its identity and privacy contact details.
